# FUTURE_DS_02
Customer Retention &amp; Churn Analysis using Excel Dashboard
Customer Retention & Churn Analysis (Task 2)
Objective:
The objective of this project is to analyse customer retention patterns, identify churn behaviour, and understand key drivers of customer loyalty using transactional data.

Tools Used:
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables
Data Cleaning & Transformation
Dashboard Design

Key Insights:
Approximately 25% of customers churn, indicating a significant retention challenge.
Customers with only one purchase show the highest churn rate, highlighting weak early engagement.
Retention improves significantly as the number of orders increases.
High-frequency customers contribute more consistently to long-term revenue.

Churn & Retention Analysis:
A large portion of customers churn after limited interaction.
Customers who make repeat purchases are far more likely to remain active.
Retention is strongly correlated with purchase frequency.

Recommendations:
Implement targeted campaigns to convert first-time buyers into repeat customers
Introduce loyalty programs to increase customer engagement
Focus retention strategies on high-value and repeat customers
Re-engage inactive customers early through personalised marketing
Improve customer experience to reduce early-stage churn
